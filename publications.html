A Mechanically Verified Garbage Collector for OCaml
Journal of Automated Reasoning, Springer · May 2025

Sheera Shamsu et al. First fully mechanized, competitive verified GC integrated with the OCaml runtime, developed in F*/Low* and compiled via KaRaMeL. The verified GC is competitive with the standard OCaml GC and the Boehm-Demers-Weiser conservative GC.

MSR Research Contributions & Collaborations

pulse-verified-gc — Verified Generational GC for OCaml
Microsoft Research · Active Collaboration · 2025

A verified generational garbage collector for OCaml 4.14, specified in F* and implemented in Pulse. Contains shared OCaml heap/object infrastructure, a verified major-heap allocator, a bounded-stack mark-and-sweep major collector, a generational collector with Cheney-style minor collection, SPOT contract audits, KaRaMeL extraction to C, and OCaml bytecode runtime integration. The active F*/Pulse tree contains 223 .fst/.fsti files and ~104k lines of source.

AVL Tree Library — Pulse Mainline Contributor
Microsoft Research · 2024

Designed, formally verified, and merged a complete AVL Tree library in Pulse into the official Microsoft Research Pulse repository. Collaborated with Senior Principal Researcher (MSR Redmond) and Principal Researcher (MSR Bangalore).

SPDM Protocol Verification
Microsoft Research Bangalore · 3-Month Research Internship · 2024

Formally verified the measurement extraction phase of the SPDM security protocol as a state machine in Pulse/Separation Logic. Overcame challenges in transcript accumulation, variable-length measurement blocks, hash computation, and signature verification. Worked directly with:

  • Nikhil Swamy — Partner Researcher, MSR Redmond; lead designer of the F* proof-oriented programming language
  • Aseem Rastogi — Senior Principal Researcher, MSR Bangalore; known for foundational work in verified low-level programming and formal software security
