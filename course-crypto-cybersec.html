Teaching Methodology & Style

Every concept earns its place by answering a question the student already has. Sessions begin with a motivating problem or analogy, build toward the formal idea, and close with a worked example — not an abstract definition. The goal is that students leave each session understanding not just what something is, but why it exists and when to reach for it.

Teaching Principles

Analogy first. Every abstract concept earns its place by solving a problem the student already cares about.

See through the structure. Before any implementation, understand the shape of the problem — what it demands for correctness, what it demands for efficiency.

No gaps. A solution is not complete until every edge case, every boundary condition, every failure mode is accounted for. This principle applies equally to proofs and to programs.
